Early Life and Career Beginnings

Alexandra Daddario was born in New York City in 1986. Growing up on the Upper East Side of Manhattan, she attended Brearley School and the Professional Children’s School. With a strong passion for acting from a young age, Daddario pursued her dreams and embarked on a full-time acting career after leaving Marymount Manhattan College.

At just 16 years old, Daddario made her television debut, kickstarting her journey in the entertainment industry. She appeared in various shows, honing her craft and gaining experience along the way. However, it was her breakthrough role as Annabeth Chase in the “Percy Jackson” film series that truly catapulted her career to new heights.

“Percy Jackson & the Olympians: The Lightning Thief,” released in 2010, marked Daddario’s first major film role. The movie’s success led to her reprising the character in the sequel, “Percy Jackson: Sea of Monsters,” in 2013.

In addition to her early success as Annabeth Chase, Daddario continued to make a name for herself in the industry. She took on diverse roles in movies such as “The Squid and the Whale” and “The Hottest State,” showcasing her versatility as an actress.

Daddario’s early life and career beginnings laid a strong foundation for her future success. Her dedication, talent, and innate passion for the craft propelled her forward, setting the stage for an impressive career in film and television.
