Career Beginnings and Breakthrough

Dej Loaf, originally known as Deja Trimble, kickstarted her music career in 2011 under the stage name Dej Loaf. Hailing from Detroit, she quickly made waves in the local rap scene with her debut mixtape, “Just Do It.” Her unique style and raw talent caught the attention of fellow rapper SAYITAINTTONE, who signed her to his record label IBGM.

However, it was in 2014 that Dej Loaf truly skyrocketed to success with her breakthrough single, “Try Me.” The track took the music world by storm, reaching the coveted Billboard Hot 100 chart and earning a Gold certification from the RIAA.

As her debut single gained traction, Dej Loaf solidified her place in the industry with her distinctive voice, captivating storytelling, and confident delivery. Her authentic lyrics resonated with fans worldwide, propelling her career to new heights.

Dej Loaf’s early success served as a springboard for her subsequent musical projects, allowing her to explore a range of genres and collaborate with renowned artists. Further Mixtapes and EPs

After her breakout success, Dej Loaf’s career continued to flourish with a series of mixtapes and EPs that showcased her unique style and versatility in the rap and hip-hop genres.

Following her signing with Columbia Records, Dej Loaf released her second mixtape, “Sell Sole,” in 2014. The mixtape received critical acclaim and further solidified her position in the music industry. It featured notable tracks such as “Bird Call” and “We Good.”

In 2015, Dej Loaf released her debut EP, “…And See That’s the Thing.” The EP peaked at number six on the Billboard Top Rap Albums chart and highlighted her lyrical prowess and ability to craft catchy hooks. Tracks like “Like a Hoe” and “Back Up” showcased her unique blend of rap and R&B elements.

Throughout her career, Dej Loaf has continued to release compelling mixtapes and EPs. Dej Loaf’s musical releases also include the mixtape “It’s a Set Up!” (2019), which showcased her versatility in delivering hard-hitting rap verses and introspective tracks. “No Saint,” her most recent project released in 2021, further solidified her standing as an artist to watch.
