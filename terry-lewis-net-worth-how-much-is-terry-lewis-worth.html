Terry Lewis Early Life and Career Beginnings

In this section, we will explore the early life and career beginnings of Terry Lewis, the renowned American R&B songwriter and record producer.

Terry Lewis was born on November 24, 1956, in Omaha, Nebraska, United States. From a young age, he had a deep passion for music and a natural talent for creating melodies. Inspired by the vibrant music scene around him, Terry decided to pursue a career in the industry.

While still in high school, Terry formed a band called Flyte Tyme, showcasing his musical abilities as a bassist. This early venture laid the foundation for his future success. The band later changed its name to The Time and gained recognition for their dynamic performances.

“Music has always been a part of my life. It’s my way of expressing myself and connecting with others.”

The Time had a breakthrough opportunity when they were given the chance to open for the legendary musician Prince on tour. This experience exposed Terry to larger audiences and solidified his determination to pursue a career in the music industry.

Building on his early success, Terry Lewis partnered with talented musician Jimmy Jam to form a powerful songwriting and record production team. Together, they founded Perspective Records, a label that would serve as a platform for their creative endeavors.

Equipped with their exceptional musical abilities and innovative production techniques, Terry Lewis and Jimmy Jam quickly gained recognition as sought-after collaborators. They embarked on a fruitful journey, producing hits for acclaimed artists such as Janet Jackson, TLC, and Mariah Carey.
