Amber Shark Tank Founder, Net Worth, and Investment

Publish date: 2024-09-04

Amber Shark Tank, a prototype mobile phone charging station, made a splash on Season 6, Episode 1 of the popular TV show. However, despite their pitch and efforts to address concerns, the sharks did not see enough potential in the product or business plan, resulting in no deal being made on the show.

The Concept of Amber Charging Stations

Amber charging stations provide a secure and convenient solution for phone charging in public places. These stations are strategically placed in various locations such as bars, malls, airports, movie theaters, and arenas, ensuring easy access for customers. With Amber charging stations, customers can simply drop their phones into an empty compartment, lock it using a fingerprint scan, and their phones will start charging automatically.

This innovative concept addresses concerns about cybercrime and data theft by offering a high level of security. Customers can charge their phones without worrying about the safety of their personal information. The charging stations are designed to provide a secure environment for phone charging, giving users peace of mind.

Besides security, Amber charging stations offer additional benefits such as customization options. Businesses that lease these stations have the opportunity to showcase their branding and advertisements, effectively reaching their target audience. The charging stations can be customized according to the specific needs of each business, creating a unique and engaging experience for customers.

Overall, Amber charging stations revolutionize the way people charge their phones in public places. By combining convenience, security, and branding opportunities, these stations are setting a new standard for public phone charging.

The Founders of Amber

Bill Shuey and Kyle Byrd are the innovative co-founders behind Amber Phone Charging. They embarked on their entrepreneurial journey in 2014 when they developed the concept for the charging stations while studying at James Madison University in Virginia. Bill, armed with a bachelor’s degree in political science, currently thrives as a home improvement sales representative. Kyle, on the other hand, leverages his bachelor’s degree in industrial design as a Senior Product Manager at a start-up.

Sharing a passion for start-up businesses, Bill and Kyle identified a market gap for phone charging stations. Their experiences at James Madison University coupled with their entrepreneurial spirit shaped the inception of Amber Phone Charging. Determined to provide a solution to the prevalent problem of phone battery depletion in public spaces, they embarked on their mission to create a safe and convenient charging system.

Bill and Kyle’s diverse skill sets and shared vision played a crucial role in the formation and success of Amber. With Bill’s sales expertise and Kyle’s product design knowledge, they brought their concept to life and developed a product that catered to the evolving needs of smartphone users.

The Amber Phone Charging concept was a testament to their innovative thinking and dedication to enhancing the tech-savvy lifestyle. By identifying an untapped market for secure phone charging solutions, Bill and Kyle demonstrated their ability to think outside the box and offer a product that addressed a growing need.

The Shark Tank Pitch

Bill and Kyle made their highly-anticipated appearance on Shark Tank Season 6 Episode 1 with hopes of securing a $200,000 investment in exchange for a 20% equity stake in their company, Amber. They zealously pitched their innovative product, emphasizing the secure and convenient features of their charging stations. The sharks, however, expressed reservations about the design and reliability of the fingerprint scanner. Despite their best efforts to address these concerns and lower the price, a deal remained elusive. In the end, no shark saw enough potential in Amber’s product and business plan to make an investment.

The sharks were hesitant about the design, particularly the reliability of the fingerprint scanner. We tried to address their concerns and lower the price of the product, but sadly, it wasn’t enough to secure a deal on the show. It was disheartening, but we were determined to move forward and prove the potential of Amber in the market.

After Shark Tank

After their appearance on Shark Tank, Amber faced numerous challenges and eventually went out of business in 2015. The portrayal of their product on the show, coupled with the criticism from the sharks, significantly impacted the perception of potential customers. However, co-founders Bill Shuey and Kyle Byrd did not allow this setback to discourage them. They swiftly transitioned to new ventures, utilizing the knowledge and experience gained from their time with Amber to propel their entrepreneurial journeys forward.

They learned to be mindful of their presentation style, ensuring that they exuded confidence and clarity.

Overall, their experience on Shark Tank provided Bill and Kyle with valuable insights into the world of entrepreneurship, public perception, and the significance of product-market fit. These lessons continue to shape their current ventures and have strengthened their ability to navigate the business landscape.
