Mensch on a Bench Shark Tank Founder, Net Worth and Investment

Publish date: 2024-07-20

Mensch on a Bench, the Jewish alternative to the popular Christmas toy Elf on the Shelf, has captured the hearts of families looking to incorporate their own traditions during the holiday season. The mastermind behind this unique concept is Neal Hoffman, an entrepreneur who appeared on the hit reality show Shark Tank to pitch his innovative product.

Hoffman’s journey began while shopping with his son, who wanted an Elf on the Shelf. As a Jewish family, they wanted a toy that would celebrate their own heritage. Inspired by this, Hoffman created the concept of Mensch on a Bench – a plush figure accompanied by a book that would bring the magic of Jewish traditions to life.

When Hoffman stepped into the Shark Tank, he sought a $150,000 investment for a 10% stake in his company. Despite initial skepticism from the Sharks, he managed to secure a deal with two prominent investors – Lori Greiner and Robert Herjavec. This pivotal moment marked the start of Mensch on a Bench’s incredible success story.

Since its appearance on Shark Tank, Mensch on a Bench has experienced remarkable growth. The company has expanded its product line, introducing new characters like the “Mitzvah Moose” and “Ask Bubbe – The Talking Grandma Doll.” These additions have further endeared the brand to families seeking to connect with their Jewish heritage.

In the year following Shark Tank, Mensch on a Bench generated impressive sales, surpassing $900,000 and solidifying its position in the market. The company’s success has also led to partnerships with major retailers, such as Target and Bed Bath & Beyond, further expanding its reach and accessibility.

Hoffman’s net worth has flourished alongside the growth of his company. Although the exact figure remains undisclosed, Mensch on a Bench’s valuation of $3 million in 2021 is a testament to his entrepreneurial prowess.

Mensch on a Bench has not only become a beloved toy for families but also a cultural phenomenon within the Jewish community. By offering a unique and comprehensive Jewish tradition experience, Mensch on a Bench has carved out a niche in the market, solidifying its position as the go-to choice for Jewish families during the holiday season.

Mensch on a Bench: Recreating Tradition

One day, while shopping with his son, Neal Hoffman noticed his son’s fascination with the Elf on the Shelf, a popular Christmas toy that brings holiday magic to children’s lives. As a Jewish family, they wanted a toy that would connect them to their own traditions during the holiday season – and thus, the idea for Mensch on a Bench was born.

Inspired to create a Jewish alternative to Elf on the Shelf, Hoffman envisioned a plush toy that would embody the values and spirit of the Hanukkah season. A Mensch, meaning a person of integrity and honor in Yiddish, was the perfect character to represent the essence of Jewish tradition and values.

To bring his vision to life, Hoffman launched a successful Kickstarter campaign, generating support and funding from fellow individuals who also sought a Jewish alternative to the popular Christmas toy. The funds raised allowed Hoffman to develop the Mensch on a Bench plush toy and create an accompanying book, which tells the story of Moshe the Mensch and his role in spreading Hanukkah joy.

The concept of Mensch on a Bench quickly gained attention and resonated with Jewish families who desired a toy that celebrated their own traditions. The toy’s popularity surged, capturing the hearts of children and parents alike. Mensch on a Bench became more than just a toy; it became a way for families to immerse themselves in their Jewish heritage and create meaningful holiday experiences.

“Mensch on a Bench was born out of a desire to provide a Jewish alternative to the Elf on the Shelf and allow families like mine to connect with our own traditions during the holiday season,” said Neal Hoffman, the founder of Mensch on a Bench.

Through the combination of a soft and lovable plush toy and an engaging storybook, Mensch on a Bench brings the spirit of Hanukkah into homes, serving as a reminder of the importance of Jewish customs, values, and community. It not only sparks joy and excitement among children but also allows families to create new traditions and strengthen their bond through shared experiences.
