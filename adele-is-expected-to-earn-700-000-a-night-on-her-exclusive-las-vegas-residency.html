Adele Is Expected To Earn $700,000 A Night On Her Exclusive Las Vegas Residency

Publish date: 2024-08-05

English singer-songwriter Adele will reportedly make almost $700,000 per night during her Las Vegas residency which will start this weekend. According to reports, Adele will receive a cut from the sales as well as raking in more money from the Adele merchandise which will be sold at the venue of her show. The singer will be performing about 24 concerts at the 4,200-seater venue every Friday and Saturday beginning this weekend until April 16, 2022.

The Rolling in The Deep singer will be literally rolling in some serious cash as she performs at the Caesars Palace’s Colosseum this weekend. The price range of the tickets for her show is from $950 to $12,500. The basic ticket is sold for $950 while the best seat in the house is currently selling for $12,500. Throughout the Easy on Me singer’s Las Vegas residency, she will be staying at Ceasars private suite which costs about $41,000 per night. The room comes with an executive assistant, security, chauffeur and a butler.

Other than the money and perks Adele will receive for her residency, the perks will be extended to her family as all of her guest such as family and friends would be able to eat and drink at any of the Caesars resort for free. Reports say that Adele is definitely the hottest ticket at the moment in Las Vegas and she will be raking in solid cash from it. Caesars also made sure that the British singer will be treated like a superstar. The album 30 which Adele released in November has become the fastest selling album of the year both in the UK and US. The first track on the album, Easy on Me has broken the record of the most streamed song in a day on the streaming app Spotify with about 24million global streams.

Aside from Adele, a lot of singers have made cash from their previous Las Vegas residencies. The highest money made in Las Vegas residencies’ history belong to the famous singer Celine Dion who’s able to rake in about almost $565 million for her residencies titled A New Day. Celine Dion’s residency ran for four years, from 2003 until 2007. Rod Steward on the other hand reportedly have made about $1,600,000 from his residency in 2012 and Lady Gaga made about $75 million for her residency in 2017. Other singers who also raked in some serious cash from Las Vegas residencies are Britney Spears, Bruno Mars, Jennifer Lopez and a lot more.
