Filippo Inzaghi is a retired Italian professional football player and manager with an estimated net worth of $2 million.

Filippo Inzaghi started his career in 1991 when he joined the Piacenza Calcio youth club. He appeared in two games with the team, and was loaned to Leffe in 1992. While with the Leffe, he contributed 13 goals in 21 matches.

He moved to other teams such as Parma and Atalanta. While with the Parma, he only did 2 goals in 15 matches. Then in Atalanta, Inzaghi received the Serie A Young Footballer of the Year award. He transferred to Juventus in 1997. During his tenure with the team, he was the top goalscorer for three consecutive seasons and played in 122 matches where he made 58 goals.

After four seasons with the Juventus, Inzaghi signed a long-term contract with the AC Milan. He appeared in 300 matches from 2001 to 2012 and contributed 126 goals to the team. On July 24, 2012, he announced his retirement from playing football, and decided to focus on his coaching career in Milan, instead.

Filippo Inzaghi was born on August 9, 1973 in Piacenza, Italy.
